1. Dataset Overview

      Total samples: 1000

      Total features: 25

      Dataset type: Numeric-encoded version of UCI German Credit dataset

      All categorical variables are encoded as numeric codes (1,2,3…)

      Target variable:

      1 → Good credit

      2 → Bad credit

      This dataset aims to predict customer creditworthiness using demographic, financial, and loan-related features.

4. Feature Insights (Based on Charts)

Below are the insights extracted from your histograms + bar charts.

  4.1 Status_existing_checking_account (Bar Chart)

          Values: 1, 2, 3, 4
          Distribution:

          4 is highest (~400 customers)

          1 and 2 moderate (~270 each)

          3 is rare (~60)

          Interpretation:

          Category 4 likely represents customers with healthy/balanced checking accounts.

          Strong financial stability indicator.

          One of the most important predictive features in credit scoring.

  4.2 Duration_months (Histogram)

          Ranges from 4 to 72 months

          Heavy clustering between 6–24 months

          Few loans past 36–60 months

          Interpretation:

          Most customers prefer short-term loans.

          Long durations increase risk → more uncertainty.

  4.3 Credit_amount (Histogram)

          Skewed distribution

          Many small loans

          Fewer large loans (long tail)

          Interpretation:

          Typical real-world loan pattern.

          Large loans naturally involve more default risk.

          Model should capture non-linear relationships (tree models handle this well).

  4.4 Housing (Bar Chart)

    Categories:

      1 → Own home (~950 customers)

      2 → Rent (~50 customers)

    Interpretation:

      Majority own homes → more financial stability.

      This feature might correlate with lower credit risk.

  4.5 Job (Bar Chart)

      Mostly categories 1 and 2

      Category 4 is rare

      Interpretation:

      Job stability plays a medium-level role.

      Higher job code generally means better employment conditions.

  4.6 Foreign_worker (Bar Chart)

      Almost all customers are category 1

      Category 2 almost negligible

      Interpretation:

      Feature may not strongly contribute to predicting risk.

      Very low variance reduces its predictive power.

  4.7 Purpose (Top 10 Only)

        Full purpose column contained 100+ small unique codes (numeric encoded).
        A full bar chart was cluttered and meaningless.

        Cleaned Approach:

        Only plotted top 10 most common Purpose codes.

        Interpretation:

        Only a few purpose codes dominate the dataset.

        Numeric version does not map to original categories (car, education, furniture).

        Still useful as a differentiator in customer intent.
